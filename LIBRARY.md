# HWFWM System Library

## System Mechanics
### Ranks
The progression system is divided into major ranks. Each rank represents a significant leap in power.
- **Normal**: Rank 0
- **Iron**: Rank 1
- **Bronze**: Rank 2
- **Silver**: Rank 3
- **Gold**: Rank 4
- **Diamond**: Rank 5

### Attributes
Characters have 4 main attributes:
- **Power**: Physical strength and damage.
- **Speed**: Movement and reaction time.
- **Spirit**: Magical power and mana pool.
- **Recovery**: Health regeneration and stamina.

### Ability Generation
Abilities are awakened by combining an **Essence** with an **Awakening Stone**.
- **Essence**: Determines the elemental flavor and tags (e.g., Fire, Dark).
- **Awakening Stone**: Determines the function (e.g., Attack, Defense).
- **Template**: The system selects an ability template that matches the Stone's function and the Essence's tags.

## Lore

### Artifacts

#### Null-Silk
Null-Silk is a rare fabric that dampens magical energy. Used by System Auditors and sensitive individuals to block out background mana radiation. It is expensive and difficult to weave, as magic cannot be used in its creation. It is harvested from the Null-Spiders found in the deepest, magic-dead zones of the Underdark.
> **Visual Prompt**: Grey, matte fabric, smooth texture, absorbing light.

#### The Glass Shield
The Glass Shield is a legendary artifact said to be forged from a single piece of transparent diamond by the Titans. It is invisible until struck, at which point it flashes with blinding light. Its current location is unknown, though many claim to have seen it in the hands of a wandering mute knight. Legends say the shield cannot be broken by any force in this world, only by a weapon forged in the Void.
> **Visual Prompt**: A warrior holding an invisible shield, impact ripple of light visible, epic stance.

### Bestiary

#### Ancient Bat
Ancient Bats are distinct from normal giant bats due to their immense lifespan and size. Living for centuries in the deepest, most mana-rich caverns, they can grow to have wingspans of thirty feet. They have developed a form of sonic magic, using echolocation screams that can shatter stone and rupture eardrums. They are solitary hunters, swooping down from the cavern ceilings to snatch up large preyincluding unsuspecting adventurers.
> **Visual Prompt**: A massive bat hanging from a cave ceiling, leather wings, dark atmosphere.

#### Ancient Sentinel
Ancient Sentinels are towering constructs left behind by the Aethel to guard their secrets. Powered by an eternal core, they do not rust or decay, standing vigil for thousands of years. They are armed with beam weaponry that can cut through Dragon scale, and their armor is resistant to most modern magic.
> **Visual Prompt**: A giant robot/golem made of white stone and gold metal, standing still in ruins.

#### Blood Devil
Blood Devils are small, red-skinned demons that manifest from confluences of chaotic blood mana. They stand roughly three feet tall but possess strength disproportionate to their size. They are driven by a frenzy for fresh blood, which they can smell from miles away. When wounded, they do not flee; instead, they enter a berserker state, attacking faster and harder. They often swarm in groups of twenty or more, overwhelming larger prey through sheer numbers and ferocity.
> **Visual Prompt**: A small, grotesque red demon with sharp claws and teeth, jumping.

#### Bronze Drake
Bronze Drakes are lesser cousins of the true dragons, found nesting in the cliffs of the badlands. Their scales are metallic and oxidized, possessing the hardness of bronze. Unlike their fire-breathing kin, Bronze Drakes possess a specialized gland that produces a highly corrosive acid gas. They are territorial and hoard shiny objects, often decorating their nests with the armor of fallen adventurers. Their hide is prized by smiths for creating lightweight, acid-resistant armor.
> **Visual Prompt**: A drake with bronze-colored scales, wings spread, green gas leaking from mouth.

#### Crystal Golem
Crystal Golems are semi-sentient constructs native to the Crystal Caverns. They are not built; they grow from the rock when mana density reaches a critical point. Their faceted bodies reflect magical attacks, making them highly resistant to spells. They attack by firing concentrated beams of light from their cores or by bludgeoning with their diamond-hard fists. Shattering a Crystal Golem yields valuable mana crystals, making them a prime target for miners despite the danger.
> **Visual Prompt**: A large golem made of jagged crystals, glowing core, lumbering forward.

#### Iron Wolf
Iron Wolves are a mutated species of canine native to the metallic mountains of the north. Over generations of consuming ore-rich prey and water, their fur has incorporated trace metals, granting them a coat that acts as natural chainmail. Their claws are razor-sharp and can shear through standard leather armor. They hunt in coordinated packs, using complex flanking tactics. An Alpha Iron Wolf can grow to the size of a horse and is a formidable opponent even for a seasoned knight.
> **Visual Prompt**: A wolf with metallic grey fur, glowing yellow eyes, snowy background.

#### Magma Crab
Native to volcanic regions, Magma Crabs have shells made of cooling lava rock. They are surprisingly fast despite their heavy armor and can spew molten rock at attackers. Their meat is considered a delicacy, said to taste like spicy lobster, but preparing it requires a chef skilled in removing the toxic fire sacs.
> **Visual Prompt**: A crab with a shell of glowing black rock and lava, volcanic background.

#### Mana Moths
Mana Moths are small, harmless insects that are naturally attracted to high concentrations of magic. They are often used by adventurers as a primitive danger sense; if a swarm of Mana Moths suddenly disperses, a predator or a mana storm is likely approaching. Their wings are coated in a fine dust that is used in the creation of invisibility potions, making them a target for low-level poachers.
> **Visual Prompt**: Swarm of small glowing moths, blue and purple wings, forest night background.

#### Mechanical Knight
Mechanical Knights are ancient automatons left behind by the Aethel civilization. Powered by eternal mana cores, they still patrol the ruins of their creators, following orders given thousands of years ago. They are armed with high-frequency vibration blades and integrated energy shields. Unlike modern golems, they possess combat algorithms that allow them to adapt to an enemy's fighting style. ### History

They were not beings of biology, but of pure concept: Stasis, Entropy, Depth, and Silence. They shaped the mountains not with hands, but with slow, geologic thoughts. For eons, the world existed in a state of perfect, unchanging equilibrium. There was no life, no death, only existence. Modern Chronomancers believe that this era still exists deep within the planet's core, a pocket of frozen time where the laws of thermodynamics do not apply.

The equilibrium was shattered by an intruder: The Solar Spark. Whether it was a wandering star captured by gravity or a deliberate creation of a higher power, no one knows. Its ignition was violent and absolute. Light flooded the surface, banishing the eternal night. The Primordials, pained by this aggressive energy, retreated to the Core, the Deep Trenches, and the Shadow Planes. In their wake, the warmth of the sun reacted with the leaking essence of the retreating Primordials. From this chaotic mixture of solar fire and primal concept, the first biological life emerged. It was messy, fleeting, and vibranteverything the Primordials were not.

They built the Floating Cities of Arcanum to be closer to the stars and further from the primitive earth. They mastered individual Essences, then pairs, creating the first Confluences. Disease was eradicated. Hunger was a memory. They believed they had surpassed the limitations of the material world, referring to themselves as the 'Architects of Fate'.

They constructed the Omega Spire, a massive focusing lens designed to draw mana from the planet's core and the sun simultaneously. They intended to overwrite the laws of physics, making themselves eternal gods of a new reality. They did not understand that some laws are load-bearing, and pulling them out would bring the roof down on everyone.

The resulting resonance didn't just explode; it shattered the dimensional barrier. The Omega Spire imploded, creating a tear in the fabric of the worlda wound that would later be known as the Void Rift. The backlash caused the Floating Cities to fall from the sky, raining debris like meteors across the continent. The Aethel were instantly vaporized or twisted into the first Horrors, their souls fused with the very magic they sought to master. The civilization that had lasted ten thousand years ended in a single heartbeat, leaving behind only the 'Sky-Fall' ruins that adventurers plunder today.

These communities are often immune to scrying magic and live in 'dead zones' where the System interface flickers and fails.
> **Visual Prompt**: A rustic village shrine with simple stone idols, no magical glow, peaceful and quiet.

### Society

#### Grand Archon Solus
Grand Archon Solus is the current leader of the Arcane Assembly. A mage of unknown age who specializes in Light and Time magic. Rumors say he is actually a construct projected from the Ancient Spire, as he has not been seen to eat or sleep in forty years. He advocates for the complete regulation of all magic usage and is the primary architect of the Assembly's restrictive policies on 'forbidden' essences.
> **Visual Prompt**: A stern wizard with glowing eyes, floating slightly off the ground, wearing robes of starlight.

#### High King Thorian
High King Thorian is the ruler of Ironhold. A giant of a man, even by mountain standards. He wields the hammer 'Earth-Shaker', a weapon so heavy that no other man can lift it. He despises politics and prefers to solve disputes through trial by combat. Under his rule, Ironhold has become isolationist but impregnable, turning the mountain into a self-sustaining fortress city.
> **Visual Prompt**: A massive warrior king with a beard, holding a giant hammer, sitting on a throne.

#### Matriarch Verna
Matriarch Verna is the head of the Church of the Healer. A kind-faced elderly woman who is secretly a Diamond-rank Life Mage. She is the only person in the world known to have resurrected someone who had been dead for over a year. She acts as the moral compass of the Council of Six, often mediating disputes between the more aggressive factions.
> **Visual Prompt**: An elderly woman with a gentle smile, wearing high priestess robes, glowing aura of warmth.

#### The Council of Six
The governing body of Greenstone City is the Council of Six. Composed of representatives from the major guilds: The Adventurer's Guild, The Merchant's Coalition, The Arcane Assembly, The Iron Vanguard, The Church of the Healer, and a representative for the Common Folk. They meet in the Emerald Hall to decide on trade tariffs, defense budgets, and city laws. Debates are known to be heated, often requiring magical suppression fields to prevent 'accidental' duels between the militant Vanguard and the arrogant Assembly.
> **Visual Prompt**: Six distinct figures sitting around a circular table in a grand hall with green banners, fantasy politics.

#### The Hawksley Lineage
The clan is currently led by Lord Alaric Hawksley (Gold Rank), a stoic spear-master known for his absolute silence in the field. His wife, Lady Seraphina (Silver Rank), manages the clan's intelligence network. Their heir, Garrick (Bronze Rank), is often criticized for his lack of natural talent but is respected for his relentless work ethic. The true prodigy is the younger daughter, Elara (Bronze Rank), who awakened a Rare 'Sight' ability at age ten. The family's 'black sheep' is Vane Hawksley, Alaric's brother, who was exiled for using poisoned baita violation of the clan's code that demands a 'clean kill' and respect for the natural order.
> **Visual Prompt**: A family portrait of noble hunters, father with a spear, mother with a map, son and daughter looking determined.

#### The Iron Court
The Iron Court is the absolute monarchy of Ironhold. While King Thorian sits on the throne, his decisions are advised by the Iron Courta collection of generals and smith-lords. Access to the court is determined by one's contribution to the Kingdom's armory or defenses. In Ironhold, political power is directly proportional to military utility. They view the democratic processes of Greenstone as weak and inefficient.
> **Visual Prompt**: A dwarven/human king on an iron throne surrounded by armored generals, stone hall, fire braziers.
